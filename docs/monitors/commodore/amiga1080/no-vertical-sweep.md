# How to Repair the Commodore Amiga 1080 Monitor: No Vertical Sweep

**Editor's Note:** A collapsed raster indicates a total failure of the vertical deflection circuit. This is an urgent issue that can burn a permanent line into the CRT phosphor if left on too long.

## 1. Technical Overview: Vertical Deflection

The vertical output circuit pulls the electron beam up and down the screen. This requires a +42V power source and a distinct sawtooth waveform generated by Q501.

## 2. Symptoms: What does "No Vertical Sweep" look like?

* A single, bright horizontal white line across the center of a black screen.

## 3. ⚠️ Critical Safety Warning

Turn off the monitor immediately if you see a single bright line. Leaving it on will cause permanent phosphor burn-in on the CRT glass.

## 4. Required Tools for the Bench

* Oscilloscope
* DMM

## 5. Step-by-Step Troubleshooting Flowchart

| Measurement Result | Likely Culprit | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Power +42V is not applied | Power/Flyback | Check Vert. out circuit. |
| Sawtooth waveform missing at #30 of Q501 | V. HOLD Circuit | Check V. HOLD circuit and Q501. |
| Sawtooth waveform missing at #28 of Q501 | Height Circuit | Check Height circuit and Q501. |

![Amiga 1080 CRT Drive Board](images/1080A_CRT DRIVE BOARD PW5252-2.png)
*Figure 2: CRT Drive Board - Vertical deflection yoke connections*

## 6. Repair Conclusion & "Recap" Advice

Check the vertical output transistors (2SC2073 / 2SA940) and verify the +42V supply line. If the sawtooth wave is dead at Q501, the IC or its surrounding capacitors have failed.

![Amiga 1080 Video Terminal Board](images/1080A_Video Terminal Board.png)
*Figure 3: Video Terminal Board - Vertical sync signal processing*

## 7. FAQ: Pro-Level Calibration

**Q: How do I adjust vertical height once repaired?**

A: Turn the V. HEIGHT control (R352) until the picture height equals 182 ± 5 mm.
